# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Chinese tax and finance practitioners, accountants, enterprise tax operators, reviewers, and administrators who need policy-valid answers with clear source traceability. They use the product while answering real tax questions, uploading official source documents, reviewing chunk quality, and auditing historical answers.

## Product Purpose

Caishui WebApp is a Chinese tax knowledge-base Q&A system. Its purpose is to make official tax policy retrieval, answer generation, source ingestion, chunk review, and citation auditing reliable enough for professional workflows. Success means users can distinguish current applicable policy from expired or unsupported material, see the evidence behind every answer, and govern source documents without losing audit history.

## Brand Personality

Calm, rigorous, and transparent. The interface should feel professional and trustworthy, with enough warmth to reduce anxiety around complex policy work.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic SaaS landing page, a decorative AI chat demo, or a dashboard that hides errors behind vague success states. Avoid heavy gradients, glass cards, oversized marketing hero sections, unexplained icons, and any design that makes unverified or withdrawn content look equally authoritative.

## Design Principles

- Evidence first: surface citations, verification, retrieval status, and failure reasons where decisions are made.
- Calm density: keep professional information compact and scannable without becoming visually noisy.
- State honesty: loading, failed, withdrawn, limited-evidence, and no-evidence states must be visible and specific.
- Audit-safe by default: UI copy should favor withdrawal, traceability, and review over destructive action.
- Familiar controls: use standard forms, tables, cards, and status badges so tax professionals can focus on the work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Do not rely on color alone for status. Support keyboard focus states and reduced motion. Use clear Chinese labels for domain actions and avoid ambiguous abbreviations in user-facing workflow states.
